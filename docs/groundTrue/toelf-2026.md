# 2026 TOEFL iBT Format & Reading Details

The TOEFL iBT was heavily updated in **January 2026**. The exam shifted to an adaptive, ~90-minute format featuring shorter, practical tasks and a new 6-band scoring system aligned with European (CEFR) standards.

---

## 1. Section Breakdown & Timing

The Reading and Listening sections are **adaptive**, meaning the difficulty of the second module adjusts based on your performance in the first. 

| Section | Total Time | Number of Questions | Est. Time Per Question |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Reading** (Adaptive) | 18–27 minutes | 35–48 items | ~30–45 seconds |
| **Listening** (Adaptive) | 18–27 minutes | 35–45 items | ~30–45 seconds (plus audio time) |
| **Writing** | 23 minutes | 12 items | Variable (short sentences vs. paragraphs) |
| **Speaking** | ~8 minutes | 11 items | ~40–45 seconds (includes prompt time) |

*Note: There are no scheduled breaks in the 90-minute format.*

---

## 2. Reading Section: Deep Dive

The high number of questions in a short time is manageable because the definition of an "item" has changed. You are no longer solely reading massive academic lectures.

### A. "Complete the Words" Task
You are given a short academic paragraph (70–100 words). Starting with the second sentence, the second half of every alternating word is missing. You type the missing letters.
* **Time:** 1–2 minutes total.
* **Item Count:** 1 blank = 1 question (e.g., one paragraph can contain 10 questions).
* **Example:**
    > The university library will be extending its hours during the final exam period. This cha___ is desi____ to he__ students fi__ a qu___ place dur___ their ex___ week be____ the str___ deadlines appr____.
* **Answers:** cha**nge**, desi**gned**, he**lp**, fi**nd**, qu**iet**, dur**ing**, ex**ams**, be**fore**, str**ict**, appr**oach**.

### B. "Read in Daily Life" Task
Highly practical, non-academic texts (15–150 words) followed by 2–3 straightforward multiple-choice questions.
* **Time:** < 2 minutes total.
* **Example Text:** A short email about scheduled pipe maintenance in a dormitory.
* **Example Question:** *What alternative is provided for students who need water?*

### C. "Read an Academic Passage" Task
The traditional university-level text, but heavily abridged.
* **Length:** Roughly 200 words.
* **Questions:** 5 multiple-choice questions per passage.

---

## 3. Other Section Tasks

### Listening
* **Listen & Choose a Response:** Single pragmatic sentences or mini-interactions.
* **Listen to an Announcement:** Campus updates and everyday situations.
* **Conversations & Lectures:** Peer dialogues, student-professor discussions, and short academic talks.

### Writing
* **Build a Sentence:** Unscrambling or creating grammatically correct sentences.
* **Write an Email:** Drafting a response in a campus or everyday context.
* **Academic Discussion:** Stating and supporting your opinion in a simulated online classroom forum.

### Speaking
* **Listen & Repeat:** Hearing a prompt and repeating it to test pronunciation and rhythm (no prep time).
* **Virtual Interview / Mini-prompts:** Short, rapid-fire responses to a simulated interviewer on familiar topics.

---

## 4. Score Matrix & CEFR Alignment

The classic 0–120 scoring scale is being phased out for a **1.0 to 6.0 band scale** (in 0.5 increments). Your overall score is the average of your four section scores. 

During the 2026–2028 transition period, score reports will display both scales.

| CEFR Level | English Proficiency | New TOEFL Band (1.0–6.0) | Traditional TOEFL Score (0–120) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **C2** | Mastery / Expert | 6.0 | 114–120 |
| **C1** | Advanced | 5.0 – 5.5 | 95–113 |
| **B2** | Upper-Intermediate | 4.0 – 4.5 | 72–94 |
| **B1** | Intermediate | 3.0 – 3.5 | 42–71 |
| **A2** | Elementary | 2.0 – 2.5 | 20–41 |
| **A1** | Beginner | 1.0 – 1.5 | 0–19 |
